11/23/22

Today I came across Sergey Prokudin-Gorsky, a Russian photographer-chemist who traveled across the Russian Empire in the early 20th century to take color photographs of its people and landscapes. Prokudin-Gorsky made use of the "three-color method," wherein black-and-white photos are taken through red, green, and blue filters. The Library of Congress undertook a project in 2000 to digitize Prokudin-Gorsky's photo triplets and later to reconstruct color images based on these. Check out the results here. Very neat stuff.
