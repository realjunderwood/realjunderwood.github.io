Central Ohio Book Exchange Program   

As the coronavirus pandemic countinues to disrupt our lives, it's a great time to relax and take out that long-neglected reading list. And while our libraries offer digital ebook collections, nothing beats the classic ink-on-the-page book. So how can we read phsyical books that we don't own? That's where the Central Ohio Book Exchange comes in. Here's how it works:

  1. 1 Anyone can check out a book from the catalogue.
  2. 2 The book's owner is alerted and places the book on their porch.
  3. 3 The borrower picks it up.
  4. 4 After two weeks, the borrower returns the book at the owner's house so that others can enjoy it.
  5. 5 Borrowers are encouraged to lend out their own book collection.

coronavirus considerations

Given the current situation, all patrons and lenders should exercise precaution when using this service. We ask that books are wrapped in cardboard and sprayed with disinfectants. Borrowers should wait 24 hours before retrieving books.

about

The Central Ohio Book Exchange Program (COBEP) was founded by UAHS student James Underwood in April of 2020 in response to the closure of local libraries. The project aims to facilitate the safe exchange of books between Franklin County residents. Users of the service can borrow and lend books to and from their neighbors.
